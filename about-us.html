Our Vision & Mission

The primary purpose of the CHANGE cooperative is to foster social and economic development for marginal farmers (men and women) in rural areas of Uttarakhand, protect the environment, and prevent animal violence. We aim to achieve this by:

  • Promoting organic farming and natural techniques.
  • Eliminating middlemen and ensuring fair markets for GI-tagged products nationally and internationally.
  • Establishing and managing domestic cottage industries for value addition to local produce.
  • Developing alternative energy sources, particularly solar energy projects.
  • Preventing animal exploitation and violence, and protecting stray animals.
  • Making farming attractive to youth through smart technology and entrepreneurship.
  • Providing microcredit, microfinance, and insurance services for financial inclusion.
  • Engaging in policy advocacy to create a supportive ecosystem for small farmers.

Our Core Objectives

  • Sustainable Agriculture: Promoting organic and natural farming methods while reducing chemical use, and providing certification services like soil health cards and organic certification.
  • Value Addition & Marketing: Enhancing traditional agricultural products (e.g., millets, pulses, herbs) through processing, packaging, and marketing, including G.I. Certified and Organic India/PGS Certified items.
  • Cottage Industries & Rural Enterprises: Establishing ventures for processing forest produce, making puja materials, vegan products, and developing ecotourism initiatives like homestays and agrotourism.
  • Animal Welfare: Actively working against animal exploitation and violence, and supporting the protection of stray animals.
  • Renewable Energy: Focusing on solar energy projects, including solar farms on unused land, to contribute to environmental protection and economic development.
  • Youth Engagement & Education: Inspiring youth to participate in agriculture through modern technology and entrepreneurial skills, and advocating for vocational subjects in schools.
  • Financial Inclusion: Offering microcredit, microfinance, and insurance to rural communities and women's Self-Help Groups (SHGs) to promote savings and self-reliance.
